# Tampa Bay Coastal Data

Local data for Tampa Bay and Hillsborough County, sourced from credible Florida agencies and regional programs. For use in CoastalCleans content to establish local relevance and urgency.

---

## Tampa Bay Estuary Program (TBEP) — Trash Free Waters

**Program:** Trash Free Waters initiative, implemented 2019–present
**Partners:** 5 partner organizations including TBEP
**Coverage:** Pinellas, Manatee, and Hillsborough Counties (CoastalCleans' home county)

**Key findings:**
- 12 litter collection devices deployed across the three counties
- **Primary finding:** Reducing single-use plastics should be the #1 strategy for controlling litter in the Tampa Bay watershed
- Data produced a **Litter Management Plan Template** adopted by other Gulf communities
- Plastics are the dominant litter type in Tampa Bay waterways

**Primary source:** [TBEP Trash Free Waters](https://tbep.org/trash-free-waters/)

## Why Tampa Bay Specifically

Tampa Bay is one of Florida's largest and most ecologically diverse estuaries:

| Fact | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Location | Hillsborough, Pinellas, and Manatee Counties, FL |
| Size | ~400 square miles of open water |
| Key species | Sea turtles, West Indian manatees, bottlenose dolphins, seahorses, pelicans, ospreys |
| Watershed | Over 2 million people live in the Tampa Bay watershed |
| Coastline | Miles of beaches, mangroves, and seagrass beds |

## Florida Marine Debris Context

From Florida Department of Environmental Protection (DEP):
- Discarded fishing and boating materials, plastics, and household items are increasingly common on Florida reefs
- Marine debris causes physical damage to sponges and corals
- Entanglement documented in fish and reef organisms

**Source:** [Florida DEP Marine Debris Fact Sheet](https://floridadep.gov/rcp/coral/documents/fact-sheet-marine-debris)

## The Land-to-Sea Pipeline

80% of ocean plastic begins on land. For Tampa Bay:
1. Plastic litter → storm drains → Tampa Bay tributaries (Hillsborough River, Alafia River) → Tampa Bay → Gulf of Mexico
2. CoastalCleans beach cleanups directly intercept plastic **before** it disperses into open water
3. Every pound removed on a Hillsborough County beach is a pound that won't enter the Gulf

> [!success] CoastalCleans' Strategic Value
> Shoreline and beach cleanups are the most cost-effective point of intervention. Removing plastic from beaches prevents it from breaking into microplastics in the water — where it becomes far harder to capture.

## Messaging Framework for "Why Tampa Bay"

Use these talking points in web content:

1. **Our backyard** — Tampa Bay is home to species that depend on clean coastlines
2. **The watershed** — 2M+ people live in the watershed; everyone contributes to the problem and can contribute to the solution
3. **The pipeline** — land → drain → river → bay → Gulf; cleanups work because they interrupt this chain
4. **TBEP validation** — a federally partnered research program confirmed that coastal plastic removal is the right strategy for this region
5. **Local action = global impact** — Tampa Bay feeds the Gulf of Mexico; clean coastlines here reduce Florida's contribution to ocean plastic
