# Marine Life Statistics — Ocean Plastic

Verified statistics from credible .gov and .org sources for use in CoastalCleans web content. All claims must link to their source.

> [!warning] Usage Rule
> Only use statistics from the sources listed here. Do not paraphrase in a way that inflates numbers. Always display the source citation on the page.

## Scale of Ocean Plastic Pollution

| Stat | Value | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| Annual plastic entering oceans (2016) | 11–23 million metric tons | EPA / NOAA |
| Floating marine debris that is plastic | 60–80% | NOAA Marine Debris Program |
| Ocean plastic that originates on land | ~80% | NOAA |
| U.S. plastic waste produced (2016) | 42 million metric tons | EPA |
| U.S. plastic entering coastal environment (2016) | ~1.45 million metric tons | NOAA |
| Economic loss per ton of plastic in ocean | $3,300–$33,000 annually | NOAA / peer-reviewed |

**Primary sources:**

## Composition of Marine Debris

- **60–80%** of all marine debris is plastic
- **90%+** of all floating debris particles are plastic
- The 10 most common items at the 2018 Ocean Conservancy International Coastal Cleanup were **all single-use plastic**:
  - Cigarette butts, food wrappers, single-use cutlery, beverage bottles, bottle caps, grocery bags, lids, cups and plates

## Plastic Never Biodegrades

Key fact for mission messaging:
> "Once in the water, **plastic debris never fully biodegrades.** It only breaks down into smaller and smaller pieces called microplastics — which are then ingested by marine life at every level of the food chain."

Source: [NOAA Microplastics Fact Sheet](https://marinedebris.noaa.gov/fact-sheets/microplastic-marine-debris-fact-sheet)
