From One Beach to Many

Our History

What started as three friends and a pickup truck has grown into Tampa Bay's most active coastal cleanup organization.

Timeline

How we got here

Every milestone traced back to people who showed up on a Saturday morning.

January 2025
CoastalCleans is founded

Maria Santos, David Chen, and Priya Nair organize the first official cleanup on St. Pete Beach. Fourteen volunteers collect 340 pounds of plastic in three hours. The group starts a group chat that night.

14 volunteers · 340 lbs
2025
Mar
March 2025
First corporate partnership

A local marine supply company sponsors a Clearwater Beach event, providing 200 pairs of gloves and branded bags. The event draws 67 volunteers and gets picked up by a St. Pete news blog.

67 volunteers · 1,200 lbs
May 2025
First quarterly mega-event

CoastalCleans hosts its first large-scale community cleanup at North Beach, St. Pete. Over 120 volunteers spend five hours clearing 2,800 pounds of debris, including abandoned fishing nets recovered by a dive team.

120 volunteers · 2,800 lbs
May
Aug
August 2025
School education program launches

Priya leads the first "Plastic Lifecycle" presentation at a Hillsborough County middle school. The program is adapted into a curriculum used at three schools by the fall semester.

November 2025
340 volunteers, 47 events

By the end of CoastalCleans' founding year, the Tampa Bay Chapter has run 47 cleanup events, removed over 12,400 lbs of trash, and built a volunteer network spanning four counties.

340 volunteers · 12,400 lbs

Where We Are

Tampa Bay Chapter

Currently active in the Tampa Bay region — with plans to expand across Florida's Gulf Coast and beyond.

Tampa Bay Chapter — Currently the only active CoastalCleans chapter. Covering Pinellas, Hillsborough, Pasco, and Manatee counties.

Looking Ahead

Chapters on the horizon

CoastalCleans is built to grow. Every chapter is locally led, locally funded, and guided by the same three pillars: Clean, Protect, Educate.

Gulf Coast FL

Fort Myers / Naples

Southwest Florida's beaches are among the most biodiverse in the state. A chapter here is in early planning.

Atlantic Coast FL

Daytona / Jacksonville

The Atlantic side brings different plastics challenges — stronger currents mean more debris. We're scouting chapter leads.

Gulf Coast MS/AL

Biloxi / Mobile

Coastal Mississippi and Alabama have limited existing cleanup infrastructure. High-impact opportunity for our second state.
